# Phrase Library

A collection of phrases, sentences, and language patterns for meditation and breathing apps.

## Breathing Instructions

### Phase Guidance

**Inhale:**
- "Breathe in"
- "Breathe in..."
- "Inhale"
- "Breathe in fully"
- "Take a breath in"
- "Breathe in through your nose"

**Hold:**
- "Hold"
- "Hold..."
- "Pause"
- "Gently hold"
- "Rest here"

**Exhale:**
- "Breathe out"
- "Breathe out..."
- "Exhale"
- "Let it go"
- "Release"
- "Long, slow exhale"

**Note:** The ellipsis (...) creates a softer, more invitational feel than a period.

### Opening Instructions

> "Begin by taking several long slow deep breaths, breathing in fully and exhaling fully."

> "Notice how your breath flows in and out. Make no effort to change your breathing in any way."

> "Taking a deep full breath in, and a long, slow exhale, allowing your eyes to gently close."

> "Don't try to control your breath in any way. Observe and accept your experience in this moment without judgment."

### Guidance Philosophy

> "Anchor Breathing teaches you how to use breathing as a way to anchor your thoughts in the present. The primary goal is to describe mindful breathing as merely a calm, non-judging awareness, allowing thoughts and feelings to come and go without getting caught up in them."

## Facilitation Notes

### Voice Guidelines

From meditation script research:

> "Use your natural teaching voice, rather than an artificial 'relaxing' voice."

> "Pause regularly. Leave space for students to shift their focus inward."

> "We suggest taking a three-second pause between each line."

### Pacing Principles

- Slow and steady
- Natural breathing pauses
- Space between instructions
- Never rushed or robotic

---

## Do's and Don'ts

### DO Use

- Simple, clear words
- Invitational language ("allow", "notice", "let")
- Present tense
- Warm but not saccharine tone
- Breathing metaphors

### AVOID

- Jargon and technical terms
- Commands without softeners
- Religious language (unless appropriate for audience)
- Overly flowery prose
- Anything that sounds artificial
